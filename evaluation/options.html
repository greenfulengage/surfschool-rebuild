What we can do - options

surfschool.com rebuild + booking automation. Discussion options, not a fixed quote.

1. The website

A. Lean rebuild (recommended)

Rebuild as a fast, hand-built static site. Near-instant load, modern feel, full ownership of the files, no WordPress and no proprietary lock-in. Keeps the TuriTop booking widget exactly as is. Hosting drops to near-zero.

B. Add a self-edit layer

On top of the rebuild, a simple editor so common changes (prices, dates, text) can be made without touching code. Optional - depends whether you want to edit yourself or have changes managed for you.

C. Tidy the existing WordPress site instead

Lower-ambition option: fix the current site in place (booking buttons, the hidden page, structured data) without a full rebuild. Cheaper now, but keeps the slow stack and the lock-in. Not recommended, but it's on the table.

2. Design direction - what others do well

Three reference sites, each strong in a different way. Worth a look to decide the feel.

M&M Surf School (Seal Beach, since 1995) surfingschool.com

Clean and restrained. Big full-width photo panels, one message each, simple lesson grid. Heritage + simplicity. Closest to the genuine, established feel of an '86 brand.

Maroochy Surf School (Sunshine Coast) maroochysurfschool.com.au

Video hero, then a tiled menu of lessons each tagged with an age range and a Book button. Excellent way to present a range and segment beginners / teens / intermediate / private at a glance.

Go Surf Perth (same beach - the direct rival) gosurfperth.com

The one to answer. Their edge is depth, not looks: a huge, genuinely useful FAQ that helps them rank, plus booking automation (self-reschedule, night-before weather confirm by email + text, customer login). They also openly target kids and corporate.

Recommendation: M&M-style restraint for the feel, Maroochy-style age-tagged lesson tiles for the range, and beat Go Surf on FAQ depth and review visibility.

4. Booking management

A. Automate around TuriTop (lowest risk)

Keep TuriTop as the booking engine. Layer automation on top: new bookings auto-populate the run sheet (review/edit step), confirmation emails drafted ready to send, day-before reminders sent off the run sheet - all with your oversight.

Needs a check of what TuriTop connects to (API / webhooks / Zapier) before committing to specifics.

B. Bespoke booking dashboard (the bigger option)

A simple booking-management screen built for you: see all bookings, daily run sheet, per-student history and course progress, and send confirmations / reminders from one place. You own it and manage it. More work to build, but it's exactly your process rather than a generic tool.

C. Switch booking engine

The rival uses Viking Bookings, which does self-reschedule and automatic weather confirm/cancel by text out of the box. Only worth it if those features matter more than staying with the familiar TuriTop. A separate, later decision.

5. Extra ideas to pull in new customers

The current site is missing a lot that people actually want before they book. These are cheap, high-appeal adds - all mobile-first.

Tell the story - an "About" page

There's no About page right now, and it's the thing people most want to see. The world's oldest surf school, family-run since 1986 - a story most competitors can't touch. Add real photos of the team and the van, a short founder story, and ideally a 60-second video. People book people, not logos.

Put the reviews on the page

512 five-star Google reviews and 195 on Tripadvisor, and not one appears on the site. Pull a rolling set of real testimonials onto the homepage and lesson pages. It's the most persuasive thing a first-timer sees, and it's already earned.

Live surf + weather widget

There's a whole Surf Conditions page but no live data. Add a simple widget showing today's Scarborough swell, wind and tide, plus a short forecast. Genuinely useful, gives people a reason to come back, and ties straight into "book an early session for the best conditions."

Pull in Instagram + Facebook automatically

The accounts are quiet and split across two Instagram handles. Two moves: consolidate to one handle, then feed that account straight onto the website so a live photo wall keeps the site looking active with no extra work. Post once, it shows everywhere - fresh social proof on autopilot.

Gift vouchers, front and centre

Big gift-buyer and tourist demand - birthdays, Christmas, Father's/Mother's Day. The rival pushes vouchers hard; the current site barely mentions them. Make them prominent and easy to buy online.

Other smart, low-effort adds

  • Embed the existing YouTube videos properly - a lesson in action sells better than a photo
  • A short "what to expect / what to bring" first-timer section - removes nerves, lifts bookings
  • Tap-to-call and tap-for-directions buttons on mobile (most traffic is phones)
  • Click-to-WhatsApp or SMS for quick enquiries
  • Email capture ("a heads-up when conditions are good") for people not ready to book yet
  • Accreditation + O'Neill / Surfing Australia badges shown as trust marks

All of these aim at the one goal: more new customers. Quick to add, and they make the site feel alive and trustworthy rather than static.

Suggested path

  • Lean rebuild + SEO/structured data first - fast, visible win, no risk to bookings
  • Then booking automation around TuriTop, with oversight
  • Bespoke dashboard and any engine change treated as later, separate decisions
